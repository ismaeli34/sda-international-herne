Foot Washing

(Ordinance of Humility)

“For I have given you an example, that you should do as I have done to you.” (John 13:15)

Foot Washing known as the Ordinance of Humility is a sacred act of service practiced before the Lord’s Supper in the Seventh-day Adventist Church. It symbolizes Christ’s humility and His command that believers love and serve one another. Through this ceremony, participants prepare their hearts for Communion with a spirit of forgiveness, unity, and renewal.

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

1. Why does the SDA Church practice Foot Washing? {{ panelOpenState() ? 'expand_less' : 'expand_more' }}

Foot Washing is practiced because Jesus washed His disciples’ feet as a lesson in humility, love, and service. It prepares believers spiritually for Communion by encouraging reconciliation, forgiveness, and renewed unity.

2. Who can participate in Foot Washing? {{ panelOpenState() ? 'expand_less' : 'expand_more' }}
  • All baptized Seventh-day Adventist members are welcome to participate.
  • Visitors who understand the meaning of the ceremony may also participate.
  • Baptized children may join with parental guidance.
 3. How is Foot Washing conducted at SDA International Church Herne? {{ panelOpenState() ? 'expand_less' : 'expand_more' }}

The church provides separate areas for men, women, and families. Participants wash one another’s feet in pairs, followed by prayer and reflection before entering the Communion service.

4. How often is Foot Washing conducted? {{ panelOpenState() ? 'expand_less' : 'expand_more' }}

Foot Washing is conducted every time Communion is held. At the International SDA Church Herne, this typically takes place once every quarter. For exact dates, please contact the church office.

5. What should I wear? {{ panelOpenState() ? 'expand_less' : 'expand_more' }}

There is no specific dress code. Participants may wear modest and comfortable clothing suitable for kneeling. The church provides all necessary materials.

6. Can families participate together? {{ panelOpenState() ? 'expand_less' : 'expand_more' }}

Yes. The church arranges a special area for families who choose to participate together as a household.

7. Why is it done before Communion? {{ panelOpenState() ? 'expand_less' : 'expand_more' }}

Foot Washing encourages humility, forgiveness, and reconciliation—helping believers prepare their hearts for the sacred Lord’s Supper.

8. How long does the ceremony last? {{ panelOpenState() ? 'expand_less' : 'expand_more' }}

The Foot Washing service typically lasts 20–30 minutes, followed by the Communion service.
